# Slide 1: Opening Hook -- Options

Pick one. The hook sets the tone for the entire talk. It should be about THEM, not about you.

---

## 1. The live threat

Open ChatGPT on the projector. Type "build me a website with a login page." Watch it spit out working code in 10 seconds.

"That used to be someone's first job. Now it's free. So... what do you do about that?"

Theme: the junior job is gone, so now what?

---

## 2. The phone audit

"Pull out your phone. Look at the last 5 apps you used."

Pause while they actually do it.

"Someone built every single one of those. Not a company -- a person. A person who at some point didn't know how to code at all. The question isn't whether you CAN build something like that. It's whether you care enough to."

Theme: their phone, their habits, their potential.

---

## 3. The TikTok algorithm

"You ever notice TikTok knows what you want to watch before YOU know? That's not magic. That's about 200 lines of math deciding what you're going to feel next. Someone your age in ten years is going to build the thing that replaces TikTok. Does anyone in this room want that to be them, or do you just want to keep scrolling?"

Theme: their daily experience, their agency over tech they use passively.

---

## 4. The honest question

"Real talk. How many of you are in this class because you actually want to be here?" Some hands. "How many are here because it fit your schedule?" Laughs, some hands. "Both of those are fine. But I want to tell you something nobody's going to say to you today: the world you're about to graduate into is completely different than the one your teachers trained for. And this room might be the most important one you're sitting in."

Theme: their situation, their skepticism, validates both reasons for being there.

---

## 5. The Minecraft question

"Has anyone here ever built something in Minecraft or Roblox that you were actually proud of? Like genuinely proud -- showed your friends, spent hours on it?" Hands go up. "That feeling -- that's the entire career. That's all it is. The tools get more powerful, the stakes get bigger, but the feeling of 'I made this and it works' is exactly the same. Except someone pays you six figures for it."

Theme: their creative experience, reframed as professional.

---

## 6. The AI panic

"So I work with AI every day. And I know you've all heard that AI is going to take every job and we're all doomed and whatever." Eye rolls. "Can I tell you what actually scares me about AI? It's not that it's too smart. It's that it has zero taste. It can write code all day and it has no idea if what it built is good, or useful, or something anyone actually wants. You know who does know that? You."

Theme: their anxiety, directly addressed, then flipped.

---

## 7. The group text

"Imagine your group chat right now. Someone sends a link to an app -- 'yo try this.' You tap it, it's fun for 30 seconds, you send it to three more people. By tomorrow it has 10,000 users. That's how every app you've ever loved started. Not in a boardroom. Not with a business plan. Somebody built something their friends thought was cool. That's literally it."

Theme: their social world, demystifies how products start.

---

## 8. The two futures

"In about two years, you're going to be making one of the biggest decisions of your life -- what to do after high school. And right now, in 2026, there are basically two kinds of people in tech. People who use AI tools, and people who build them. The first group is everyone. The second group is tiny, getting paid more every year, and they can't hire fast enough. Which group do you want to be in?"

Theme: their immediate future, their decision, framed as a choice not a lecture.

---

## 9. The creator vs. consumer

"Quick question. How much time did you spend on your phone yesterday?" Let them think. "Now how much of that time were you making something versus watching something someone else made?" Silence. "That ratio -- maker versus consumer -- is the single biggest predictor of where you'll be in ten years. And the cool part is you can change it today."

Theme: their behavior, no judgment, empowering.

---

## 10. The unfair advantage

"You guys have an unfair advantage over every generation that came before you, and nobody's told you about it yet. When I started coding, if I got stuck I had to read a 600-page book and hope the answer was somewhere in chapter 14. You can ask an AI and get an answer in 3 seconds. The ceiling for what you can build is basically gone. The only question left is: do you actually want to build something?"

Theme: their generation, reframes AI as their superpower not their threat.

---

## 11. The vote with your feet

"Forget me for a second. I want to know what YOU think." Put three statements on the projector. Have them physically stand and move to a corner of the room.

- "AI is going to take most jobs" (corner A)
- "AI is just a tool, humans will be fine" (corner B)
- "I honestly have no idea" (corner C)

"Great. By the end of this hour, I want you to see if you've changed your mind. And I'm going to be honest with you -- the answer isn't any of these three."

Theme: their opinion, physical movement, stakes for paying attention. Has a built-in callback for the closing.
