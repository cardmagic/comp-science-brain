# Audience Research -- High School CS Students (2025-2026)

## Demographics and Mindset

**Age:** 14-18 (9th-12th grade)
**Tech relationship:** Digital natives. Nearly half are online "almost constantly." Over 80% of rising seniors have used generative AI tools. About 85% play video games.

## What Excites Them

- **AI as a creative tool** -- they want to build with it, not just use ChatGPT
- **Making things** -- games, music, animations, apps, websites, electronic gadgets
- **Career opportunities in AI/tech** -- global AI market expected to reach $190B in 2025
- **Tech intersecting with other fields** -- arts, humanities, social sciences + CS
- **Feeling capable** -- "I built that" is the most powerful feeling in a CS class

## What Worries Them

- **Job displacement by AI** -- the dominant anxiety among this generation
- **Whether education is worth it** -- growing frustration that school doesn't prepare them
- **Climate change** -- 58% of youth 16-25 are "very" or "extremely" worried
- **Mental health and social media** -- they know the platforms affect them
- **Privacy** -- aware their data is collected, feel powerless to stop it
- **Not belonging in CS** -- especially female students, non-traditional backgrounds

## Gender Gap Note

Male students are noticeably more optimistic about AI than female peers (ACT research). Worth being aware of in how you frame AI content -- don't assume universal excitement.

## What They Respond To in Speakers

- **Authenticity over polish** -- they can smell inauthenticity instantly
- **Vulnerability** -- share failures, not just wins
- **Practical relevance** -- "how does this affect MY life and MY future?"
- **Interactivity** -- being talked AT for an hour is torture
- **Respect** -- treating them as future colleagues, not kids to inspire
- **Humor** -- natural, not forced
- **Brevity** -- get to the point, don't ramble

## Questions They Will Ask (Be Ready)

- "How much money do you make?" (salary is the #1 student question to guest speakers)
- "Do I need to go to college for this?"
- "Will AI replace programmers?"
- "What language should I learn?"
- "How did you get your first job?"
- "What do you actually do all day?"
- "Is it boring?"
- "Do you work from home?"
