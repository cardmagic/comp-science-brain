# Speaker Background -- Mapped to Student Relevance

## The Story Arc

Lucas Carlson has been coding since the mid-1990s. The arc that matters for students:

1. **Started young, learned by doing** -- built web apps starting in 1996
2. **Wrote books** -- O'Reilly programming books (teaching others is a career)
3. **Built something that became Apple Music** -- wrote the streaming code, holds a patent Apple still uses
4. **Started a company** -- AppFog, raised $10M in venture capital, sold to CenturyLink
5. **Open source** -- projects downloaded 9+ million times
6. **Current role at Zapier** -- teaching AI how to use APIs in the real world

## What's Relatable to Each Audience

### Intro to CS Students
- **"I started where you are"** -- everyone begins not knowing anything
- **Open source = sharing your code helps millions** -- their capstone projects are the start of this
- **Writing books about code** -- you can teach others what you just learned
- **Building things people actually use** -- Apple Music started as code, not a boardroom presentation

### AP CSP Students
- **How the internet works in practice** -- streaming music is about protocols, data, compression
- **Impact of computing** -- Apple Music changed how billions consume music (Big Idea 5)
- **Entrepreneurship as computing** -- AppFog was a platform-as-a-service (relates to Big Idea 4, systems/networks)
- **AI and APIs** -- Zapier work directly connects to their understanding of how computing impacts society
- **Ethics and decisions** -- selling a company, open source vs proprietary, AI safety

### AP CSA (Java) Students
- **Professional software engineering** -- this is what the career actually looks like
- **Patents and intellectual property** -- code has legal and business value
- **Startup engineering** -- building production systems under pressure
- **Open source at scale** -- maintaining code that millions depend on
- **AI engineering** -- teaching AI to use APIs is a cutting-edge Java/programming challenge
